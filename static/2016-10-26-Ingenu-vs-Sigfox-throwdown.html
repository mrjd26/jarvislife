Internet of Things Cellular networks

I've been following the nascent Internet of Things for about two years now and have seen great offerings in the form of wifi chips, namely by a company called Particle, but there are probably others.

I first became aware of a San Diego company, Ingenu at a meeting space here in Austin where they were showing how they were rolling out a nationwide IoT network and Austin was one of the first cities to get this network deployment. What was immediately interesting to me was that this seemed like the cellular solution that has been much needed in the IoT space. If you haven't checked out Ingenu I would highly recommend it, as it seems they have all the ingredients of success from the pitch they gave here at the tech meetup

Having been aware of Sigfox since sometime around 2014, I immediately thought about their network and had even met a French-American engineer during tech meetup and he had mentioned Sigfox's network

My story would end here if it hadn't been an interest of mine for a long time now to make some kind of prototype project that runs on cellular network IoT

i My IoT water meter that is talking to a Particle chip on home wifi

After attending the Ingenu meetup, I tried to get one of their development boards which they refer to as an rACM. I emailed back and forth with a member of their business solutions team about being able to pay $150 for this board which they claim to be plug n play much like an arduino. It got weird when I had been approved to buy a board from Ingenu and they proceeded to send me an NDA to sign, (which I did) in order to get the development board. I don't hold it against Ingenu, but I think they need to contact the people at Particle and work out a partnership of some kind. It seemed like Ingenu was kind of struggling to get their development board and all the associated documentation user communtiy and forms etc. up and running. I'm still hopeful that someday I will be able to test out their network on some kind of easily accessible and programmable chip like an Arduino development board that just seemlessly talks to the cell tower. It was kind of funny how I kept on emailing Ingenu and someone kept on promising to sell me this development board. They told me that the current development board, the rACM, doesn't work indoors yet and also that the price would be $150 and there would be no engineering support should I run into any problem. I ultimately said to myself, " well I guess I can program this development board with a linux computer on my driveway despite it being the height of summer here in Austin, and that well despite its $150 price tag, it would be worthwhile to try out their network and build something on it just to get going with it" but as stated, the board never came and I just got tired of trying to contact them about it.

Enter Sigfox

I thought well, Ingenu may take some time for it to get up and running for people like me who just want to build stuff on their network and not lose my mind in the process. So I finally got a chance to check out Sigfox. I jumped on their site, where there was a way to contact Sigfox. It looked like, they kind of stole a page out of the Ingenu playbook when they state on their website they are planning to roll out a network in 100 cities in the United States after just hearing this from the Ingenu road show. Either way its a good thing, and I filled out a form asking about the availability of the network here in Austin.

I also filled out some kind of questionnaire about whey I was interested in Sigfox's network. Well I learned when a Sigfox employee emailed me back that the network is not in Austin. So that kind of put a damper on getting my little IoT water meter totally remote and off the wifi network. Interestingly though I was contacted again by some people involved from the 'IoT Valley' in Toulouse,France. Supposedly, trying to make this area of the world a hub for IoT activity. These people from something called the 'Le Connected Camp' asked what my idea or business idea was about and if I would be interested in submitting it to their accelerator program which is what Le Connected Camp is. This never really panned out into anything material, which was fine with me as I beileve they want people to move and work out of Toulouse France, which I don't know could be worked out even if the money was available for my family to do such a thing.

I'll probably do a part 2 (part deux!) on the status of the water meter. Its undergone quite a transformation since inception and is begging for a revisit as I see it sitting on the shelf next to me!
