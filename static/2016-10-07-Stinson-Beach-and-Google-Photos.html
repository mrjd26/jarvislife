A day at Stinson Beach

I'm switching all of our family photos to Google Photos from Dropbox to save $10 a month, the price I'm currently paying dropbox to house all of our pictures. I saw these photos from a trip we took to Stinson Beach one day. It's about a 2-3 hours drive, depending on where you live in the bay area. The beach town is pretty nice. It's pretty low key, people playing on the beach, a few shops and restaurants and a small town behind it. I would recommend taking the drive sometime if you live in the Bay Area

We walked around the beach when we got there, walked up into town, found a little art gallery, ate lunch near the beach, and we later ended up in a place called Breakers, where we sat at the bar and I had a drink. The bartender saw my wife was like 8 months preganant and was really awesome and just gave us this little onesie for our new arrival. A bluegrass/flatpickers band was playing on the deck outside and I really didn't expect that kind of music to be played in that part of California, but it kinda fit just in that when you wander around in California you're likely to see lots of eclectic things

So if you are thinking about the same as me, transferring all your dropbox photos to google photos, unless you are really tedious with hundreds of gigabytes of data, your stuff could just get all lumped in together. What I mean is that, when uploading to google photos you have to specifically upload to an album that you have created to have parity with your dropbox account. I chose to forgoe this step and literally everything previously that was in my google photos just got a dropbox dump over the last few days. I tried the uploader 'app' but it seemed to work,.. just ... well .. okay. I found the better way was to use the browser version of google photos and upload images there. One annoyance was that you cannot just select a directory like 'dropbox2016' and have all the sub directories and sub directories of sub directories all transfer to google photos. I really don't understand why this is. So I had to take each and every directory and 'Ctrl+A' all the photos in that directory upload, then move on to the next one, etc. Its' really not to bad, as the photos all kind of queue up to get uploaded. So I would click through five directories on my computer and build this upload queue up to 1000 photos or something, and then just let the computer upload and leave and do something else. Then when i walked by the computer again later and saw it had 899 of 1199 photos uploaded, or whatever , I would add more to the queue and click through more photos.

I think generally google photos is a little harder to use than dropbox and is a little less intuitive/convenient like I was stating earlier about the photo upload process. The free photos up to 100 terabytes or whatever the limit is is awesome, but also keep in mind its photos/videos and not files. It makes sense for us though as we are, like most people accumulating 1000's of photos a month on smart phones etc. The nice thing about google photos is being able to just search though a mess of photos. When I wrote this article, for example, I just go to google photos and type in Stinson Beach. So even if you type in something like 'machinery' or 'engine' and Google has a hard time, you can usually get close with some term like 'car' or 'vehicle' and then look at the date of one of the photos from that car project I worked on 2 years ago and I'm interested in finding the picture for it
